# validate-agents ‚Äî Spec 2 Lens 3 Design (`bounded` observable / `max_abs`)

- **Date:** 2026-06-24
- **Status:** Approved design, pending implementation plan
- **Builds on:** Lens 3 v1 (`ode_integrate`) + negative-control + `linear_stability` ‚Äî the structured `SimulationPlan`, the safe expression-tree evaluator, the deterministic fixed-step RK4, the robustness sweep + structured `sim_criterion`, and the conservative attack-path gate mapping.
- **Status line:** *`bounded` lets a mechanistic claim assert its dynamics stay bounded ‚Äî and makes a genuine divergence a deliberate FAIL ‚Äî while refusing to let a fixed-step RK4 numerical blow-up launder into a physical refutation. The honesty check is dt-refinement convergence of the refuting verdict, not mere persistence of a blow-up.*
- **One-line goal:** Add a `max_abs` observable and an inverted non-finite rule scoped to the **bounded claim** (`max_abs le bound`), so a confirmed-dt-independent divergence (or an over-`bound` breach that survives refinement) **refutes** boundedness, while a step-size artifact stays **uncertain**.

---

## 1. Scope

### In scope
- A new observable **`max_abs(var, window_frac)`** = peak `|state[var]|` over the window (finite case computes like the other extractors).
- The **bounded claim** = `max_abs` with criterion **`op="le"`** (`max_abs ‚â§ bound`). For this and only this combination, the v1 non-finite‚Üíuncertain rule is **inverted under an honesty check**: a divergence (or a finite breach `> bound`) **refutes** boundedness ‚Äî but only when the refuting verdict **survives dt-refinement**.
- The **honesty check (the load-bearing piece ‚Äî ¬ß3):** "a refutation must survive dt-refinement." The discriminator is the **convergence** of the refuting quantity across halved `dt` ‚Äî the overflow *time* `t*` for a divergence, the `max_abs` value for a finite breach ‚Äî NOT mere persistence of a blow-up.
- `SimCfg.max_dt_halvings` (refinement depth) and `SimCfg.conv_rtol` (convergence tolerance) ‚Äî system-set.
- The integrator returns the overflow step index (so `t_of` can be compared across refinements).

### Out of scope (deferred / unchanged)
- `ode_integrate` only (no trajectory in `linear_stability`).
- The inverted rule is scoped to `max_abs` + `op="le"`. `max_abs` with any other `op` falls back to the **v1 rule** (finite ‚Üí value; blow-up ‚Üí uncertain) ‚Äî the mirror "exceeds/instability" claim (`max_abs` + `gt`, where a blow-up would false-*confirm*) is a deferred extension (B-D6).
- Full singularity classification beyond `t*`-convergence (e.g. order-of-blow-up fitting) ‚Äî not needed; `t*`-convergence is the v1 discriminator.
- Source grounding (Spec-3); the model/parameters remain LLM-asserted, frozen, not grounded.

## 2. The `max_abs` observable + the inverted rule (scoped)

- `max_abs(var, window_frac)` joins the observable vocabulary: peak `|state[var]|` over the last `window_frac` of the trajectory (a finite real float; `window_frac` validity as the other observables).
- **Selection of the bounded path:** the executor uses the honesty path (¬ß3) iff `observable.name == "max_abs"` AND `sim_criterion.op == "le"`. Otherwise `max_abs` behaves as an ordinary observable (finite ‚Üí value via `_extract_observable`; a non-finite trajectory ‚Üí uncertain, the v1 rule unchanged).
- **Guarded direction = false-refute.** A `BOUNDED` verdict at the base `dt` (finite, `max_abs ‚â§ bound`) is **accepted** (no refinement). *(Residual, stated in the caveat ¬ß5: a coarse `dt` could miss a transient spike ‚Üí a false-PASS; mitigated by the mandated `window_frac=1` and an adequate base `dt`, and a PASS is discounted regardless. The harmful direction for a validation system is the false-REFUTE, which ¬ß3 guards.)*

## 3. The honesty check ‚Äî a refutation must survive dt-refinement (the crux)

Per grid point, for a bounded claim (`max_abs`, `op="le"`, threshold `bound`):

1. **Integrate at `dt`** with the overflow-capturing integrator ‚Üí `(traj_or_partial, overflow_step)` (`overflow_step` = the first step at which the state **diverges** ‚Äî reaches `inf` or crosses the divergence magnitude `_DIVERGENCE_MAG`; `None` if the trajectory stays finite and bounded by it). A `nan` (a *domain* error such as `log`/`sqrt` of a negative, or `inf‚àíinf`) is **not** a divergence ‚Üí it raises ‚Üí uncertain (BP-1, ¬ß9). **Classify** the point:
   - **BOUNDED:** finite AND `max_abs ‚â§ bound`.
   - **REFUTING ‚Äî divergence:** `overflow_step is not None` (a blow-up). Record `t_of = overflow_step √ó dt`.
   - **REFUTING ‚Äî finite breach:** finite AND `max_abs > bound`. Record the `max_abs` value.
2. **BOUNDED at `dt` ‚Üí accept** (the point is bounded; no refinement).
3. **REFUTING ‚Üí refine** (`dt/2, dt/4, ‚Ä¶` up to `max_dt_halvings`). Each refinement doubles `n_steps`; if a needed refinement would have `n_steps √ó 2^k > max_steps` ‚Üí **uncertain** (cannot confirm at the available resolution). At each refinement, re-classify, and decide:
   - **Any refinement is BOUNDED ‚Üí uncertain** (the refutation vanished under refinement ‚Äî a step-size artifact).
   - **Divergence branch (overflow at the base `dt`):** the point must overflow at every refinement AND **all three** of: (a) `t_of` **converges ‚Äî monotone and shrinking** (¬ß3a); (b) to a `t*` **strictly inside the window** (`t* < t_span_end` AND `t*` not within `conv_rtol` of `t_span_end` ‚Äî ¬ß3b); (c) the **pre-overflow trajectory is `dt`-converged** (¬ß3d) ‚Üí **REFUTE** (a confirmed finite-time singularity). If the divergence vanishes/morphs to a finite breach, or `t_of` fails (a)/(b), **or the pre-overflow trajectory fails (c)** ‚Üí **uncertain**. **(a)+(b) are necessary but NOT sufficient** ‚Äî a *persistent* stiff numerical instability (`dt¬∑Œª` above the RK4 stability boundary at every refinement) has a `t_of` that converges too (¬ß3d) ‚Äî so (c) is the load-bearing addition.
   - **Finite-breach branch (finite `> bound` at the base `dt`):** the point must stay a finite breach at every refinement AND `max_abs` must **converge ‚Äî monotone and shrinking** (¬ß3a) ‚Äî to a stable value **`> bound`** ‚Üí **REFUTE**. If `max_abs` **trends down** toward `bound` (the spike tames under refinement) or never converges ‚Üí **uncertain**.

> **¬ß3a ‚Äî Convergence is monotone AND shrinking, not a single lucky pair.** "Converged" requires BOTH: (i) the refining quantity `q` (`t_of` for divergence, `max_abs` for breach) is **monotone** across the refinement sequence (`t_of` non-increasing; `max_abs` not reversing direction), AND (ii) the successive absolute deltas `|q(h/2) ‚àí q(h)|` are **shrinking** across the sequence and the latest *relative* delta `|q(h/2) ‚àí q(h)| / |q(h)|< conv_rtol`. A pure two-value rtol gate is trippable by one coincidentally-close pair on an otherwise-receding sequence; the monotone-and-shrinking requirement closes that single-lucky-sample hole (the `bounded` analog of the linstab grid's "monotone + shrinking" guard). **At least two refinements (three `q` samples) are required** before convergence may be declared; if `max_dt_halvings < 2` the feature cannot refute and every refuting point is uncertain.
>
> **¬ß3b ‚Äî `t*` near `t_span_end` is its own uncertain zone.** A finite-time singularity at `t* ‚âà t_span_end` is indistinguishable from a bounded trajectory that simply has not blown up *within the integration window* ‚Äî "diverges at `t*=0.98¬∑t_span_end`" cannot be told from "bounded, would-blow-up at `1.5¬∑t_span_end` if integrated longer." So a `t*` converging to within `conv_rtol` of `t_span_end` ‚Üí **uncertain** (extend the window and re-decide), never refute. Otherwise the verdict depends on the arbitrary choice of `t_span_end` ‚Äî exactly the free-parameter dependence the honesty check exists to remove.
>
> **¬ß3d ‚Äî The pre-overflow trajectory must be `dt`-converged (the load-bearing discriminator; `t*`-convergence alone is NOT sufficient).** The decisive question is not *when* the trajectory overflows but *what path it took there*. A divergence is real iff the numerical solution is tracking the true solution up to the blow-up: if the pre-overflow trajectory `dt`-converges and a converged trajectory then overflows, the true solution it tracks genuinely diverges. A stiff bounded artifact cannot satisfy this ‚Äî above the RK4 stability boundary each refinement is a *different* oscillating garbage sequence, orders of magnitude apart at any fixed time, so its pre-blow-up path is `dt`-**divergent**. **Procedure:** let `t_edge = min‚Çñ t_of,k` (the earliest overflow time across the refinement levels ‚Äî in the deep-stiff regime the *finest* refinement overflows earliest, so the minimum bounds the common finite window). Sample the state magnitude `m(t_s) = max·µ¢|y·µ¢(t_s)|` at **several** `t_s` spread across `(0, t_edge)` and **biased toward `t_edge`** (the approach to the overflow). At each `t_s`, require the across-refinement sequence `[m‚ÇÄ(t_s), m‚ÇÅ(t_s), ‚Ä¶]` to **agree** (relative spread `(max‚àímin)/max|¬∑| < conv_rtol`). **Converged at every `t_s` up to the overflow neighborhood ‚üπ real divergence ‚Üí REFUTE; diverges at any `t_s` ‚üπ numerical artifact ‚Üí uncertain.** Sample across the *whole window up to `t_edge`*, never a single early point: at small `t` the stiff refinements still sit near `x‚ÇÄ` and agree, so an early-only sample spuriously reads "converged" ‚Äî the divergence only shows as the instability amplifies toward `t_edge`. Including the coarse levels and requiring a tight spread errs toward **uncertain** (a missed refutation, not a false one) when the base `dt` is too coarse to resolve the trajectory ‚Äî the safe direction.

> **Why convergence-of-the-path, not persistence and not `t*` alone (the soundness argument ‚Äî checked across the FULL `dt¬∑Œª` range, not one regime).** Both a true finite-time singularity and an under-resolved stiff-but-bounded system keep blowing up as `dt` shrinks, so "blew up at every refinement" would **false-refute** any stiff-bounded system. The overflow **time** `t_of` is a *necessary* gate but is **not sufficient** ‚Äî and the artifact has **two distinct regimes** the discriminator must be sound against, both enumerated here so "sound" means "checked across the whole range":
> - **Near-boundary recede (`dt¬∑Œª ‚Üí 2.78‚Å∫`):** the instability is marginal, per-time growth `‚Üí 0`, so `t_of` **recedes** (`‚Üí ‚àû`) and then vanishes once `dt¬∑Œª < 2.78` (caught by ¬ß3a's shrinking test or the "BOUNDED at a refinement ‚Üí uncertain" escape).
> - **Deep-unstable fast-converge (`dt¬∑Œª ‚â´ 2.78`):** the instability amplifies so fast that `t_of` is **small and `dt`-converges** (decreasing, shrinking) ‚Äî e.g. `·∫ã = -1000x` at `dt=0.05` gives `t_of = [0.95, 0.625, 0.4375, 0.40]`, *indistinguishable from `·∫ã = x¬≤`'s `[‚Ä¶‚Üí1.0]` by `t_of` alone*. **This regime defeats `t*`-convergence**, and is the reason ¬ß3d (pre-overflow trajectory convergence) is required: at a fixed pre-overflow time, `·∫ã = x¬≤` gives `|x| = [2.00, 2.00, 2.00, 2.00]` (converged ‚Üí real) while `·∫ã = -1000x` gives `[3e21, 1e33, 1e46, 2e50]` (garbage ‚Üí uncertain).
>
> The combined rule (`t_of` converges in-window **AND** the pre-overflow trajectory converges) is sound against both regimes and restores the err-toward-uncertain symmetry with `fixed_point_tol`.

The result is fixed-step and deterministic (no adaptive stepping ‚Äî L3-D11 holds); the only new capability is the integrator **returning** the overflow step index.

> **¬ß3c ‚Äî Determinism/ordering invariant (the refinement branch must not break bit-reproducibility).** Refinement is new, data-dependent control flow (it fires only on a refuting point), so the Spec-2 bit-reproducible-verdict invariant is restated for it explicitly: (i) the refinement sequence is **always** `dt ‚Üí dt/2 ‚Üí dt/4 ‚Üí ‚Ä¶`, the same order every run; (ii) a grid point's verdict depends only on that point's `(params, init)` and is **independent of sweep traversal order** ‚Äî no point's refinement reads state mutated by another point; (iii) the cumulative-refinement-step budget (¬ß4) is checked against a **deterministic running total of integration steps**, never wall-clock or any timing signal. Same plan ‚Üí identical verdict including the full refinement path.

## 4. Work bounds + config

- **Per-refinement cap:** a refinement at `dt/2^k` runs `n_steps √ó 2^k` steps; if that exceeds `max_steps` ‚Üí uncertain (can't confirm).
- **Base-plan headroom (prompt-budgeted):** the base `n_steps` must satisfy `n_steps √ó 2^(max_dt_halvings) ‚â§ max_steps` ‚Äî else even one refinement exceeds `max_steps` and the feature is inert (instant uncertain on any refuting point). The designer prompt instructs budgeting this headroom.
- **Cumulative refinement budget:** refinement only fires on refuting points, but a genuinely unbounded sweep refutes at many points. The executor tracks **cumulative refinement steps across the sweep** and caps them at `max_total_steps`; exceeding ‚Üí uncertain. (The nominal `grid √ó n_steps √ó n_arms ‚â§ max_total_steps` cap is unchanged and separate.)
- **Config (`SimCfg`, system-set, injected via `_sim_ceilings`):** `max_dt_halvings: int = 3`; `conv_rtol: float = 0.1`. Added to `_REQUIRED_CEILINGS`.

> **`conv_rtol = 0.1` ‚Äî justification (it sits on the load-bearing test, like `fixed_point_tol`).** The two failure directions are asymmetric. **Too tight:** a genuine singularity whose `t_of(dt)` converges *slowly* (algebraically, not geometrically) reads as non-converging ‚Üí uncertain ‚Äî you miss real refutations, the **safe** direction (consistent with err-toward-uncertain). **Too loose:** a slowly-*receding* `t_of` looks "converged" ‚Üí false refute ‚Äî the **harmful** direction. At `0.1` we declare convergence when successive overflow times agree to 10%: for `·∫ã=x¬≤` near `t*=1` that is reached in 2‚Äì3 halvings, but a marginal stiff system whose `t_of` drifts ~10% per halving could slip through a *pure* rtol gate. The chosen value therefore biases slightly toward "converged," and the **mitigation is structural, not a tighter number**: the ¬ß3a monotone-and-shrinking requirement ‚Äî a recede produces non-monotone or non-shrinking deltas that a single-pair rtol gate can miss ‚Äî plus the ¬ß3b `t*`-near-`t_span_end` uncertain zone. `conv_rtol` is a system knob, never LLM-set.

## 5. Gate integration + the loud caveat

- **Gate mapping reused, unchanged.** A bounded **REFUTE** (confirmed divergence or confirmed breach) ‚Üí the point fails the boundedness criterion ‚Üí across the sweep, `< robust_frac` pass ‚Üí executor verdict `refute` ‚Üí `landed` ‚Üí **`challenged`**; a robust bounded **PASS** ‚Üí discounted `survived`; **uncertain** ‚Üí no-op. `verdict_to_sim_attack`, `_evaluate`, `run_simulation_checks` untouched; the basis already renders the observable (`max_abs`) + criterion (`le bound`).
- **`computed`** summarizes: e.g. `"bounded: 5/5 points within bound (frac >= 1.00)"` or `"unbounded: 2/5 points diverge/breach (confirmed dt-independent)"`.
- **Detail row** distinguishes the two non-PASS outcomes by **separate sentinels** (never `+‚àû` ‚Äî keeps the JSON finite/valid like the other rows), so an inconclusive sweep is auditable ‚Äî is the budget too tight, or is the physics genuinely divergent?
  - `"diverged"` ‚Üí a **confirmed** dt-independent divergence (‚Üí refute).
  - `"refine_budget_exhausted"` ‚Üí a refuting point that ran out of `max_dt_halvings` / step budget **without deciding** (‚Üí uncertain).
  - A finite-breach refute records the converged `max_abs` value; a vanished/receded artifact records its (finite) base-`dt` `max_abs` with `verdict="uncertain"`.
  - Row shape: `{params, init, max_abs | "diverged" | "refine_budget_exhausted", t_star?, verdict, refinements_used}`.

> **Loud caveat.** A `bounded` **FAIL** means the toy model genuinely diverges (or breaches `bound`) where the idea claims boundedness, **confirmed dt-independent** across refinements ‚Äî a serious challenge, not a step-size artifact. A `bounded` **PASS** means the dynamics stayed within `bound` across the swept range **at the chosen resolution** (a coarse `dt` could miss a spike ‚Äî the residual false-pass; `window_frac=1` and an adequate base `dt` mitigate it, and a pass is discounted regardless). As in all of Lens 3, the model/parameters are LLM-asserted, frozen, not grounded. v1.x rigor option: order-of-blow-up fitting on top of the `t*`-convergence test.

## 6. Discrimination mode (per-arm)

When `null_overrides` is set (negative-control), the honesty check + uncertain-propagation run **per-arm**. The null arm is *meant* to diverge, but its blow-up still needs dt-confirmation. An **unconfirmed blow-up in EITHER arm ‚Üí whole-run uncertain**. A point discriminates iff the mechanism arm is `BOUNDED` and the null arm is a **confirmed** `unbounded` (the mechanism is what keeps it bounded).

## 8. Testing (deterministic, real numpy)

- **Observable:** `max_abs` on a known trajectory = peak `|var|` over the window (finite case).
- **Bounded pass:** `·∫ã = -a*x` (decays), `max_abs le 2`, `window_frac=1`, swept `a>0` ‚Üí never exceeds ‚Üí `confirm`.
- **Confirmed divergence ‚Üí refute:** `·∫ã = x¬≤` (finite-time singularity at `t*=1/x0`), `t_span` past `t*`, `max_abs le 10` ‚Üí overflow with `t_of` converging across refinements AND the pre-overflow trajectory `dt`-converging (¬ß3d) ‚Üí `refute`.
- **Deep-unstable stiff artifact ‚Üí uncertain (THE soundness test ‚Äî `t_of` converges, ¬ß3d catches it):** `·∫ã = -1000x` at `dt=0.05` (truly bounded decay, `dt¬∑Œª` above the RK4 boundary at *every* `max_dt_halvings`), so it overflows at every refinement and `t_of = [0.95,0.625,0.4375,0.40]` **converges** (decreasing+shrinking) ‚Äî `t*`-convergence alone would false-refute. The pre-overflow trajectory `|x(t_s)|` is `dt`-**divergent** (`[3e21,1e33,1e46,2e50]`) ‚Üí ¬ß3d fails ‚Üí `uncertain`, NOT refute. **This is the regression test for the `t*`-vs-trajectory hole.**
- **Near-boundary stiff artifact ‚Üí uncertain:** `·∫ã = -Œªx` with `dt¬∑Œª` just above ~2.78 at the base, so a finer refinement drops below the boundary and is BOUNDED ‚Üí `uncertain` (the "BOUNDED at a refinement" escape).
- **Finite-breach survives ‚Üí refute:** a system whose true peak `> bound`, finite, `max_abs` converging `> bound` across refinements ‚Üí `refute`.
- **Finite-breach artifact ‚Üí uncertain:** a coarse-`dt` spike `> bound` that `dt/2` tames under `bound` ‚Üí `uncertain`.
- **Monotone-shrinking guard (¬ß3a):** a synthetic `t_of` sequence that is overall receding but has one coincidentally-close consecutive pair ‚Üí **uncertain** (a pure two-value rtol gate would false-refute; the monotone+shrinking predicate must not).
- **`t*`-near-`t_end` (¬ß3b):** a singularity whose converged `t*` lands within `conv_rtol` of `t_span_end` ‚Üí **uncertain**, not refute; the same system with a longer `t_span` (so `t*` is well inside) ‚Üí `refute`.
- **Budget-exhausted sentinel (¬ß5):** a refuting point that converges-undecided within `max_dt_halvings` writes `"refine_budget_exhausted"` with `verdict="uncertain"`, distinct from a confirmed `"diverged"`.
- **Determinism of the refinement branch (¬ß3c):** the same plan run twice ‚Üí identical verdict and identical detail rows; a permuted grid order ‚Üí identical per-point verdicts.
- **Work bounds:** a base `n_steps` with no refinement headroom (`n_steps √ó 2^K > max_steps`) on a refuting point ‚Üí uncertain; a sweep whose cumulative refinement exceeds `max_total_steps` ‚Üí uncertain.
- **Scoping:** `max_abs` + `op != le` falls back to the v1 rule (blow-up ‚Üí uncertain, no inversion).
- **Discrimination:** bounded-with-mechanism + confirmed-unbounded-without ‚Üí discriminating pass; an unconfirmed blow-up in either arm ‚Üí uncertain.
- **Detail/JSON:** a confirmed divergence writes the `"diverged"` sentinel, not `Infinity`; the result JSON round-trips.
- **Gate (FakeLLM designer + real executor):** confirmed-unbounded on a `load_bearing novel_core` claim ‚Üí `landed fatal` ‚Üí `challenged`; robust bounded ‚Üí discounted `survived` (`checks == []`); uncertain ‚Üí no-mark. Teeth/gate-purity pins green.
- **Determinism:** same plan ‚Üí identical verdict (incl. the refinement path).

## 9. Decision log
- **B-D1** New observable `max_abs` (peak `|var|`); the inverted non-finite rule is scoped to `max_abs` + `op="le"` (the bounded claim). Any other `op`/observable keeps the v1 blow-up‚Üíuncertain rule.
- **B-D2 (the crux ‚Äî refined by B-D8; `t*`-convergence is NECESSARY, not sufficient)** The honesty check is **dt-refinement convergence**, not persistence of a blow-up (persistence false-refutes any stiff-but-bounded system). For a finite breach the discriminator is the `max_abs` value (converges `> bound` ‚Üí genuine; trends to `bound` ‚Üí artifact) ‚Äî sound, no proxy. **For a divergence the overflow time `t_of` is a necessary gate but NOT sufficient** ‚Äî see B-D8: a *deep-unstable* stiff instability has a `t_of` that converges just like a true singularity, so the divergence branch additionally requires the **pre-overflow trajectory** to be `dt`-converged (¬ß3d). Err toward **uncertain** ‚Äî symmetry with `fixed_point_tol` restored. *(The original B-D2 framing "stiff `t_of` recedes" held only for the near-boundary regime; B-D8 corrects it.)*
- **B-D3** A refutation is triggered by ANY refuting verdict (overflow OR finite `max_abs > bound`) and must survive refinement; a `BOUNDED` verdict at the base `dt` is accepted (guarded direction is the false-refute).
- **B-D4** Bounded work: per-refinement ‚â§ `max_steps`; base `n_steps` budgeted for `2^max_dt_halvings` headroom; cumulative refinement steps capped at `max_total_steps`; the integrator **returns** the overflow step index. Fixed-step, deterministic.
- **B-D5** Gate mapping reused (refute‚Üíchallenged, pass‚Üídiscounted survived, uncertain‚Üíno-op); only `computed`/detail change (the `"diverged"` sentinel). Discrimination honesty + uncertain-propagation are **per-arm**; unconfirmed blow-up in either arm ‚Üí whole-run uncertain. `window_frac=1` + `robust_frac=1` are prompt-mandated for the standard claim.
- **B-D6 (the `gt` mirror is NOT a sign-flip ‚Äî do not "restore symmetry" later)** Deferred: the `gt` "exceeds/instability" mirror, and order-of-blow-up fitting (a v1.x rigor add on top of `t*`-convergence). **Why `gt` is a separate, harder design and not the symmetric mirror of `le`:** under `le` the artifact is a *spurious blow-up* and the honesty check demands a *refutation* survive refinement ‚Äî it filters a divergence that isn't there. Under `gt` (confirm-on-blow-up) the artifact is the **opposite**: a true-bounded stiff system that spuriously blows up would spuriously **confirm** the instability claim ‚Äî a numerical artifact manufacturing a **PASS**, the exact laundering Spec 2 exists to stop. The convergence test does **not** symmetrically rescue `gt`, for two reasons: (i) the **guarded direction flips** ‚Äî you must err toward *refute* (don't confirm instability unless the blow-up is dt-independent), the mirror image of `le`'s err-toward-uncertain; (ii) "instability onset" is frequently **not a finite-time `t*` singularity at all** ‚Äî a Hopf/Turing instability is a *bounded* growing oscillation with no overflow, so the `t*`-convergence discriminator does not even apply to the most common `gt` claim. A future `gt` slice needs its own discriminator (e.g. a growth-rate fit), not a reused `bounded` path. **Do not symmetrize `_bounded_observe` to cover `gt`; that reintroduces a false-confirm laundering path.**
- **BP-1 (overflow trigger ‚Äî what counts as a divergence vs a domain error; resolved at plan time, the spec under-specified it)** The spec said "the integrator returns the overflow step" but did not define *when* a step overflows, and the existing safe evaluator collapses a magnitude blow-up (`inf`) and a domain error (`nan` from `log`/`sqrt` of a negative) into one `ValueError`. That matters: a domain error strikes at a **dt-stable time**, so it would *converge* under refinement and could **false-refute** ‚Äî the very bug class B-D7 guards. Resolution: (a) `_eval_expr` gains `allow_nonfinite=False`; default is byte-identical (complex rejected, non-finite raises, Pow-overflow raises), and **only** the overflow-capturing integrator passes `True`, which relaxes *only* the finiteness raise (returns `inf`/`nan`) ‚Äî the complex guard and every structure/whitelist guard stay unconditional, so the security surface is unchanged. (b) A divergence = the state reaching `inf` **or** crossing a module constant `_DIVERGENCE_MAG = 1e100`; `t_of` = time to cross it. A `nan` mid-run ‚Üí **raises (`_DomainError`) ‚Üí uncertain**, never a divergence. `_DIVERGENCE_MAG` is **verdict-invariant** ‚Äî it sets only the `t_of` scale, not whether `t_of` converges vs recedes ‚Äî so it is an internal numerical constant, not a policy knob. **Why a `nan` must be uncertain (the sharp reason ‚Äî it is not "just a domain error to be safe about"):** a `nan` is a *domain* event, **orthogonal to the magnitude claim**. The bounded claim is about magnitude (`max‚Çú|x(t)| ‚â§ bound`); a domain error means the RHS became undefined at a **finite, in-bounds** state (`x` crossed zero finitely, then `log` of a negative). At the `nan`, `|x|` was finite, so the `nan` can neither confirm nor refute boundedness; the `t*`-convergence machinery does not even apply (there is no overflow time to converge). Refuting on it would **launder a well-posedness failure into a boundedness verdict** ‚Äî the exact category-confusion Spec 2 exists to stop. This is self-consistent with the check order: a genuine blow-up crosses `_DIVERGENCE_MAG` as a large finite (or `inf`) and is captured as a divergence at the *prior* step, before any `inf ‚àí inf` could produce a `nan` ‚Äî so the `nan` path cleanly isolates *true* domain errors (state finite, RHS undefined). **Diagnostic (verdict unchanged):** a domain error raises a distinct `_DomainError` carrying a `"domain_error"` label, so an inconclusive sweep can tell "model ill-posed at a reachable state" apart from "couldn't confirm divergence." **Known safe-erring edge:** a blow-up that first manifests as `inf ‚àí inf ‚Üí nan` *inside one RK4 stage* (before that step's magnitude check) is labeled a domain error (uncertain) rather than a divergence ‚Äî contrived, and errs toward uncertain.
- **B-D7 (convergence-boundary hardening ‚Äî same bug class as every prior slice)** The honesty check is pinned at its boundaries so it cannot be under-specified into a false verdict: **(a)** convergence is **monotone AND shrinking**, never a single rtol-close pair, and needs ‚â•2 refinements (¬ß3a); **(b)** a `t*` within `conv_rtol` of `t_span_end` is **uncertain**, not refute ‚Äî the verdict must not depend on the arbitrary window length (¬ß3b); **(c)** the refinement branch preserves bit-reproducibility ‚Äî fixed sequence, traversal-order-independent, step-count (not wall-clock) budget (¬ß3c); **(d)** `conv_rtol`'s bias (slightly toward "converged") is mitigated structurally by (a)+(b), not by a tighter number (¬ß4); **(e)** budget-exhausted-without-deciding gets its own `"refine_budget_exhausted"` sentinel, kept distinct from confirmed `"diverged"` (¬ß5).
- **B-D8 (the divergence discriminator is trajectory-convergence, not `t*` alone ‚Äî caught by adversarial review; THIRD instance of the under-specified-at-an-unpictured-regime bug class)** `t*`-convergence (B-D2) is necessary but **not sufficient** for a divergence: a genuinely bounded *deep-unstable* stiff system (`·∫ã = -1000x` at `dt=0.05`, `dt¬∑Œª` above the RK4 boundary at every refinement) overflows at every level with `t_of = [0.95,0.625,0.4375,0.40]` that **converges** ‚Äî verified to return a false `"unbounded"` on the as-built `t*`-only code. The sound discriminator is **¬ß3d ‚Äî pre-overflow trajectory convergence**: a real singularity's numerical solution `dt`-converges up to the blow-up (the path is real), while a stiff artifact's pre-blow-up path is `dt`-divergent garbage (orders of magnitude apart at a fixed time). The divergence branch refutes iff `t_of` converges in-window **AND** the trajectory converges (¬ß3d). **Implementation detail that is itself load-bearing:** sample trajectory agreement across the *window up to the overflow neighborhood*, biased toward `t_edge = min‚Çñ t_of,k`, **never a single early point** ‚Äî at small `t` the stiff refinements still sit near `x‚ÇÄ` and agree, so an early-only sample spuriously reads "converged." The sampling runs **up to `0.95¬∑t_edge`** (6 points): an "agree-through-X-then-diverge-in-the-last-bit" profile is the **unsafe direction** (it would FALSE-REFUTE = laundering), so the window is **closed by construction**, not left to a reachability argument ‚Äî see the meta-guard.

> **Meta / standing process guard (the real lesson ‚Äî sharpened).** This is the third time the honesty check was under-specified at a parameter regime the author had not pictured (name-collision circularity; persistence-vs-`t*`; now `t*`-vs-trajectory). All three were failures of *imagination*, not diligence ‚Äî had the regime been pictured it would have been handled. So "enumerate the regimes you checked" is necessary but **not the whole guard**: enumeration still runs through the author's imagination, the very thing that failed. What actually caught all three was **adversarial construction by a fresh agent trying to build the laundering input** (the no-ground-truth task's review, then the 36 live probes). The durable invariant: **every honesty check gets an adversarial-construction pass; the regime list is the OUTPUT of that pass, never a substitute for it.** Enumeration records what the adversary found; it cannot certify what the author did not look for. Corollary applied here: an **unsafe-direction gap** (one that could false-refute/false-confirm) is never left open on a reachability argument ‚Äî that is structurally the move that just failed three times ‚Äî it is closed by construction if the close is free (as the `0.95` sampling close was: zero-regression, 16-probe-verified). A reachability argument is only acceptable for a gap whose bound is *quantified and adversarially probed*, and even then the cheaper move is to close it. The adversarial review on the no-ground-truth task is what surfaced the original miss ‚Äî the project's own thesis (don't trust the author's say-so; make an independent adversary try to break it) turned back on its authors.
